## Overview

Single Sign-On (SSO) lets organization admins connect a corporate identity provider (IdP) so users sign in to Astralform with their existing work credentials. Astralform supports SAML with any compliant IdP, including Okta and Microsoft Entra ID.

SSO binds to an [organization](/enterprise/organizations) and to the email domain(s) that organization owns. Only an organization **owner** or **admin** can configure it, in **Organization Settings > SSO**.

## Set Up a Connection

1. Go to **Organization Settings > SSO**
2. Enter your IdP's **SAML metadata URL**
3. Enter the **email domain(s)** your organization owns (e.g., `acme.com`) — users with these domains sign in through your IdP
4. Click **Create Connection**

The connection starts in a **Pending** state. It does not activate until you prove you own the domain.

## Prove Domain Ownership

SSO only activates after you prove ownership of each email domain — this is what prevents a connection from being bound to a domain you don't control.

1. In the SSO section, the dashboard shows a **DNS TXT challenge** for each domain
2. At your DNS provider, add a TXT record at `_astralform-challenge.<domain>` (for example, `_astralform-challenge.acme.com`) with the value shown in the dashboard
3. Wait for DNS to propagate, then click **Verify domains**

Once ownership is verified, the connection becomes active.

## Configure Your IdP

The dashboard shows the **Service Provider (SP) metadata** you need to create the SAML app in your IdP:

- **SP Entity ID / metadata URL** — the service provider identifier
- **ACS (Assertion Consumer Service) URL** — where your IdP posts the SAML assertion after authentication

Paste these values into your IdP's SAML application configuration.

### Attribute Mapping

Configure your IdP to send the user's **email** and **name** in the SAML assertion. Astralform uses the email to match the user to a verified domain, and the name for their display profile.

## Sign In with SSO

Once a connection is active:

1. Go to the Astralform login page
2. Choose **Sign in with SSO**
3. Enter your **work email**
4. You're redirected to your IdP to authenticate, then back to Astralform, signed in

<Note>
  Any user with an email at a **verified domain** can sign in via SSO once the connection is active.
</Note>
